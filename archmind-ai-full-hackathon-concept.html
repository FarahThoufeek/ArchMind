01 — The Problem

Why Does This Need to Exist?

Clients come to architects and designers with folders of Pinterest screenshots, Instagram saves, and magazine clippings. Translating that visual chaos into a coherent design brief — and then into spatial reality — takes weeks of discovery meetings, manual mood boarding, and iterative back-and-forth.

Core Pain: No tool today takes a client's raw inspiration images, understands their underlying design intent, and produces a unified architectural concept — exterior form, interior spaces, materials, and mood board — in a single conversation.

User Journey

1
Client starts a project
Names the project, sets property type (villa, apartment, commercial), rough size and budget tier.
2
Upload reference board
Drag-and-drop 3–20 images. Label them: "love this", "like parts of it", or "just for mood".
3
AI extracts Design DNA
GPT-4o Vision analyses the board: style (e.g. Contemporary Arabian), palette (#E8D5B7, #2C2C2C), materials (concrete, brass, travertine), mood (open, warm, airy), and lighting character.
4
Concept generation
3 exterior renders, 3 key interior renders (living, kitchen, master), schematic floor plan, and material mood board — all in one pass.
5
Client dialogue & refinement
"I love concept A but want higher ceilings and a desert garden feel." AI updates renders and adjusts design parameters accordingly.
6
Export design package
Full brief: renders, DNA summary, space program notes, material palette with sourcing, next-steps checklist.

Key UX Principles

Reference First, Prompt Second
Inverts the usual AI pattern — show images first, then refine in words. No design vocabulary needed from the client.
Design DNA Always Visible
Client sees exactly what AI extracted. Builds trust and creates a living brief they can edit directly.
Exterior + Interior as One System
All renders share the same palette, materials, and proportional language. Coherence is enforced, not left to chance.
Progressive Disclosure
Thumbnails → full renders → floor plan → palette. Never overwhelming the client with too much at once.

Key Technical Challenges

Multi-image Style Coherence
Hard
Aggregating style from 10 diverse images without averaging into a bland result. Fix: weight by client priority labels + cluster conflicts into "primary vs accent" axes.
Exterior ↔ Interior Consistency
Hard
Ensuring the same palette appears in both render types. Fix: shared structured prompt with locked palette + material tokens injected into every generation call.
Floor Plan Generation
Medium
Generating spatially valid floor plans. Fix: fine-tuned SDXL on architectural floor plan datasets + room detection validation post-processing.
Latency in Live Refinement
Medium
Re-generating renders per chat turn is slow. Fix: progressive quality (thumbnail → full-res) via async job queue with streaming partial updates.
